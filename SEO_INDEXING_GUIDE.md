# SEO Indexing Guide - GSoC Contributor Hub

If you search for "gsoc 2026" and can't find your site yet, don't worry! This is normal for new websites. Google can take anywhere from **3 days to 3 weeks** to discover a new site naturally.

Follow these steps to "teleport" to the front of the line.

## 1. The "Official" Way (Google Search Console)
This is the single most important step for any website owner.

1.  Go to [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/about).
2.  Click **"Start now"** and log in with your Google account.
3.  **Add Property**: Enter your website URL (e.g., `https://gsoc-contributor-hub.vercel.app`).
4.  **Verify Ownership**: Follow the instructions (usually adding a DNS record or a meta tag).
5.  **Submit Sitemap**:
    - In the left menu, click **Sitemaps**.
    - In "Add a new sitemap", type: `sitemap.xml`
    - Click **Submit**.
    - *Google will now crawl every single project page automatically.*

## 2. The "Fast" Way (URL Inspection)
If you want one specific page (like your homepage) to show up faster:
- In Search Console, paste your URL into the top search bar ("Inspect any URL").
- Click **"Request Indexing"**. This tells Google to prioritize your site in the next crawl.

## 3. SEO Realities
- **Competition**: "GSoC 2026" is a highly competitive keyword. The official Google site and university blogs have years of "Trust" with Google.
- **Unique Keywords**: Try searching for `"GSoC 2026 Contributor Hub"` (with quotes). Since this is your unique brand name, you should see your site much sooner for this specific term.
- **Social Signals**: Sharing your link on Twitter, LinkedIn, and GitHub helps Google's "bots" find your site faster through those referral links.

---

> [!TIP]
> **Check Indexed Pages**: You can see exactly what Google knows about your site by searching `site:your-domain.com` in Google. If results show up, you are indexed!
